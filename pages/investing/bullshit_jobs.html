Preface: On the Phenomenon of Bullshit Jobs

Over the course of the last century, as predicted, productive jobs have been largely automated away. Rather than allowing a massive reduction of working hours to free the world's population to pursue their own projects, pleasures, visions and ideas, we've seen the ballooning of the administrative sector, up to and including the creation of whole new industries (financial services, telemarketing, corporate law, academic and health administration, human resources, public relations).

If someone had designed a work regime perfectly suited to maintaining the power of finance capital, it's hard to see how they could have done a better job:

  • Real, productive workers are relentlessly squeezed and exploited. The remainder are divided between
  • A terrorized stratum of the universally reviled unemployed and
  • A larger stratum who are basically paid to do nothing, in positions designed to identify with the perspectives and sensibilities of the ruling class. But at the same time, foster a simmering resentment against anyone whose work has a clear and undeniable social value

It's as if someone were out there making up pointless jobs just for the sake of keeping us all working. The ruling class has figured out that a happy and productive population with free time on their hands is a mortal danger.

One possible vision of hell: a collection of individuals who are spending the bulk of their time working on a task they don't like and are not especially good at.

There seems to be a general rule that, the more obviously one's work benefits other people, the less one is likely to be paid for it. Even more perverse, there seems to be a broad sense that this is the way things should be. This is one of the secret strengths of right-wing populism.

Q: What does it say about our society that it seems to generate an extremely limited demand for talented poet-musicians but an apparently infinite demand for specialists in corporate law?

A: If 1% of the population controls most of the disposable wealth, what we call "the market" reflects what they think is useful or important, not anybody else.

If a private company hired a consultant to come up with a business plan, and it resulted in a sharp decline in profits, that consultant would be fired. With free market reforms, this never seemed to happen. The more they failed, the more they were enacted. The only logical conclusion was that the economic imperatives weren't really driving the project.

Millions of people spend their working lives performing tasks they secretly believe do not really need to be performed.

We have come to believe that men and women who don not work harder than they wish at jobs they do not particularly enjoy are bad people unworthy of love, care, or assistance from their communities. It's as if we have collectively acquiesced to our own enslavement. As a result, hatred, resentment, and suspicion have become the glue that holds society together.

Chapter 1: What is a Bullshit Job?

Since there is such a thing as social value (apart from mere market value), but since no one has ever figured out an adequate way to measure it, the worker's perspective is about as close as one is likely to get to an accurate assessment of the situation. If the preponderance of those engaged in a certain occupation privately believe their work is of no social value, one should proceed along the assumption they are right.

Definition: A bullshit job is a form of paid employment that is so completely pointless, unnecessary, or pernicious that even the employee cannot justify its existence even though, as part of the conditions of employment, the employee feels obliged to pretend that this is not the case.

Kings, earls, emperors, pasha, emirs, landlors and the like might, arguably, be useless people. Many of us would insist that they play pernicious roles in human affairs. But they don't think so.

Most people who do a great deal of harm in the world are protected against the knowledge that they do so. The more harm a category of powerful people do in the world, the more yes-men and propagandist will tend to accumulate around them, coming up with reasons why they are really doing good - and the more likely it is that at least some of those powerful people will believe them.

Bullshit Jobs and Shit Jobs

Three broad categories of jobs:

  • Useful jobs (which may or not be shit jobs)
  • Bullshit jobs
  • A small but ugly group made up of people who are basically just selfish bastards and don't really pretend to be anything else (gangsters, slumlords, top corporate lawyers, hedge fund CEOs)

Bullshit jobs often pay quite well and tend to offer excellent working conditions. They're just pointless. Shit jobs are usually not at all bullshit; they typically involve work that needs to be done and is clearly of benefit to society. It's just that the workers who do them are paid and treated badly.

Shit jobs tend to be blue collar and pay by the hour, whereas bullshit jobs tend to be white collar and salaried. Those who work shit jobs tend to be the object of indignities; they not only work hard but also are held in low esteem for that very reason. But at least they know they're doing something useful.

Those who work bullshit jobs are often surrounded by honor and prestige; they are respected as professionals, well paid, and treated as high achievers (the sort of people who can be justly proud of what they do). Yet secretly they are aware that they have achieved nothing. They feel they have done nothing to earn the consumer toys with which they fill their lives; they feel it's all based on a lie, and it is. These are two profoundly different forms of oppression.

We have created societies where much of the population, trapped in useless employment, have come to resent and despise equally those who do the most useful work in society, and those who do no paid work at all.

Bureaucracies

Public and private bureaucracies have become so increasingly entangled that it's often very difficult to tell them apart. The assumption that government is necessarily top-heavy with an unnecessary levels of administrative hierarchy, while the private sector is lean and mean, is by now so firmly lodged in people's heads that it seems no amount of evidence will displodge it.

(The giant bureaucracy of the Soviet Union) is always represented as exactly what would never happen under capitalism. But, just as Socialist regimes had created millions of dummy proletarian jobs, capitalist regimens somehow ended up creating millions of dummy white-collar jobs instead.

Beside government bureaucrats, when talking about bullshit jobs many will assume you must be talking about secretaries, receptionists and various sorts of (typically female) administrative staff.

The assumption that it's mainly women who end up in bullshit jobs is not only sexist but also represents a profound ignorance of how most offices actually work. Men are far more likely to feel that their jobs are pointles (42%) than women do (32%) (the source is the YouGov survey).

There are very few jobs that don't involve at least a few pointless or idiotic elements. To some degree, this is probably just the inevitable side effect of the workings of any complex organization. The process of bullshitization is highly inconsistent. It has affected middle-class employment more than working-class employment. Within the working class, it has been traditionally female, caregiving work that has been the main target of bullshitization.

As a result, it's possible to say there are partly bullshit jobs, mostly bullshit jobs, and entirely bullshit jobs.

If 37 to 40% of jobs are completely pointless, and at least 50% of the work done in nonpointless office jobs is equally pointless, we can probably conclude that at least half of all work being done in our society could be eliminated without making any real difference at all.

Chapter 2: What Sorts of Bullshit Jobs Are There?

Flunkies

  • Flunky jobs are those that exist only or primarily to make someone else look or feel important

Throughout recorded history, rich and powerful men and women have tended to surround themselves with servants, clients, and minions of one sort of another. Assigning people minor tasks as an excuse to have them around making you look impressive has a long and honorable history.

To be an aristocrat meant to have the power to order others around. An analogous logic seems to be at work in corporate environments.

Examples of flunkies: Doormen, receptionists and front-desk personnel at places that obviously don't need them

Q: Why shell out a full-time salary and benefits for a woman just to sit at the front desk all day doing nothing?

A: Because not doing so would be shocking and bizarre. No one would take a company seriously if it had no one at all sitting at the front desk.

Managers off-load more and more of their responsabilities onto the lowest-ranking subordinate to give the impression that they were too busy to do such things themselves. Leading, of course, to their having even less to do than previously. A spiral culminating in the apparently bizarre decision to hire another manager to stare at the wall, just because hiring him would make it look like that was not what everyone else was doing. Whose job is really bullshit, the flunky's or the boss'?

The flunkies end up effectively doing the bosses' jobs for them. This was the traditional role of female secretaries (administrative assistants) working for male executives during most of the 20th century.

The middle manager staring at the wall all day may or may not have been willling to admit his job was pointless. But those hired just to make someone like him seem important almost invariably know it and resent it.

This creates an even more powerful incentive for those on top of the organizational ladder to hire more employees and only then decide what they are going to do, or to resist any efforts to eliminate jobs that are found to be redundant. For without flunkies, to whom, exactly, would they be "superior"?

Goons

  • Goons are people whose jobs have an aggresive element but, crucially, exist only because other people employ them

Example: national armed forces. Countries need armies only because other countries have armies. If no one had an army, armies would not be needed. Same of most lobbyist, PR specialists, telemarketers and corporate lawyers. Like literal goons, all have a largely negative impact on society.

Goons have bullshit jobs because so many of those who have these type of work feel their jobs have no social value and ought not to exist. Most times these jobs also involve manipulative and aggressive tactics.

"I consider a worthwhile job to be one that fulfills a preexisting need, or creates a product or service that people hadn't thought of, that somehow enhances and improves their lives. I believe we passed the point where most jobs were these types of jobs a long time ago. Supply has far outpaced demand in most industries, so now it is demand that is manufactured.

If we're at the point where in order to sell products, you have to first of all trick people into thinking they need them, then I think you'd be hard-pressed to argue that these jobs aren't bullshit."

Another example are call center employees. Most times, the work involves tricking or pressuring people into doing things that aren't really in their best interest. What really irks is

  1. the aggression and
  2. the deception.

There are few things less pleasant than being forced against your better nature to try to convince others to do things that defy their common sense.

Duct Tapers

  • Duct tapers are there to solve a problem that ought not to exist. Underlings whose jobs are to undo the damage done by sloppy or incompetent superiors

It's a term that comes from the software industry. With the growing reliance on free software, paid employment is increasingly reduced to duct taping.

Another example is cleaning. Cleaning is a necessary function. But cleaning up after someone who makes a completely gratitous and unnecesary mess is always irritating. Having a full-time occupation cleaning up after such a person can only breed resentment.

Many duct-taper jobs are a result of a glitch in the system that no one has bothered to correct - task that could easily be automated but haven't been for any reason.

On the social level, duct taping has traditionally been women's work. In a more material sense, duct taping might be considered a classic working-class function.

There will always be a certain gap between blueprints, and plans and their real-world implementation. Therefore, there will always be people charged with making the necessary adjustments.

What makes such a role bullshit is when the plan obviously is so stupidly designed that it will fail in completely predictable ways, but rather than fix the problem, the organization prefers to hire full-time employees whose entire job is to deal with the damage. Duct tapers are almost always aware they have a bullshit job and are usually quite angry about it.

Box Tickers

  • Box tickers are employees who exist only or primarily to allow an organization to be able to claim it's doing something that, in fact, it's not doing. We're all familiar with box ticking as a form of government, but large corporations will behave in the same way

The most miserable thing about box-ticking jobs is that the employee is usually aware that not only does the box-ticking exercise do nothing toward accomplishing its ostentible purpose, it actually undermines it, since it diverts time and resources away from the purpose itself.

These type of works also involves creating presentations. It's very easy to charge a large amount of money to write bullshit reports. The physical attractiveness of the reports is fundamental.

If the ongoing importance of a manager is measured by how many people they have working under them, the immediate material manifestation of that manager's power and prestige is the visual quality of their presentations and reports. The meetings in which such emblems are displayed might be considered the high rituals of the corporate world.

Taskmasters

Taskmasters fall into 2 subatgories:

  1. Those whose role consist entirely of assigning work to others. This job can be bullshit if the taskmaster believes that there is no need for their intervention, and that the underlings would be perfectly capable of carrying on with their work without them. They're unnecessary superiors rather than unnecessary subordinates
  2. Those whose primary role is to create bullshit tasks for others to do, to supervise bullshit or even to create entirely new bullshit jobs.

The entire job of middle management is to ensure the lower-level people hit their "productivity numbers", and will therefore start coming up with formal statistical metrics that their underlings can try to falsify.

It's not capitalism per se that produces the bullshit. It's managerialist ideologies put into practice in complex organizations:

  • The creation of a bullshit job often involves creating a whole universe of bullshit narrative that documents the purpose and functions of the position as well as the qualifications required to successfully perform the job, while also adhiering to the buraucratic requirements of HR
  • Once that's done, there has to be a narrative job announcement
  • To be eligible for hire, the applicant must present a resume incorporating the themes and phrasology of the announcement to be considered

It can also sometimes be clear that a job is bullshit but still be difficult to determine precisely which of the 5 categories it belongs to. Often it may seem to contain elements of several.

Only one class of people that not only denied their jobs were pointless but expressed outright hostility to the very idea that our economy is filled with bullshit jobs: business owners and anyone else in charge of hiring and firing.

Chapter 3: Why do those in bullshit jobs regularly report themselves unhappy?

"Workplaces are fascist. They're cults designed to eat your life; bosses hoard your minutes jealously like dragons hoard gold."

- Nouri

Why does having a pointless job so regularly causes people to be miserable? One might imagine that those being paid to do nothing would consider themselves fortunate, especially when they are more or less left to themselves. Many seem perplexed by their own reaction, unable to understand why their situation left them feeling so worthless or depressed.

Everyone is encouraged to assume that human beings will always tend to seek their best advantage: to find themselves a situation where they can get the most benefit for the least amount of time and effort. But our experience tends to contradict this assumption.

People almost never act and react to situations in quite the way our theories of human nature predict.

It's not just the purposelessness of the jobs, it's also the falseness. A con artist could easily take delight in what they're doing. But being forced to scam someone is altogether different. In such circumstances, it's hard not to feel you're ultimately in the same situation as the person you're scamming: you're both being pressured and manipulated by your employeer.

Nowadays it's considered important that young people should work. However, it's not considered important they should work at anything useful. They're barely expected to work at all, just to show up and pretend to do so. Efficiency is not the point.

Jobs are meant to teach:

  1. How to operate under other's direct supervision
  2. How to pretend to work even when nothing needs to be done
  3. That one is not paid money to do things, however useful or important, that one actually enjoys
  4. That one is paid money to do things that are in no way useful or important and that one does not enjoy
  5. That even when one is being paid to carry out tasks one does not enjoy, one also has to pretend to be enjoying it

Human Motivations

"Economic Man": the model human being that's behind every prediction made by economics is assumed to be motivated above all by a calculus of costs and benefits

The assumption is that everyone, left to their own devices, will choose the course of action that provides the most of what they want for the least amount of resources and efforts. This simplicity makes economic prediction possible

There's this idea that people have to be compelled to work. If the poor are given relief so they don't actually starve, it has to be delivered in the most humilliating way possible, because otherwise they would become dependant and have no incentive to find proper jobs.

The underlying assumption is that if humans are offered the option to be parasites, of course they'll take it. Almost every bit of evidence indicates that this is not the case.

The pleasure of being at the cause

German psychologist Karl Groos discovered that infants express extraordinary happiness when they first figure out they can cause predictable effects in the world, regardless of what the effect is or if it could have any benefit to them. Scientists had been inclined to assume that humans seek power because of an inherent desire for conquest or domination, or for purely practical desire to guarantee access to physical safety, gratification or reproductive success.

But Groos found that children come to understand that they exist, that they are entities separate from the world around them, largely by understanding that "they" are the thing which cause something to happen.

Experiments have also shown that if one first allows a child to discover and experience the delight of being able to cause a certain effect, and then suddenly denies it to them, the results are dramatic:

  • rage, refusal to engage
  • then a kind of catatonic folding in on oneself and withdrawing from the world entirely

Humans invent games and diversions for the exact same reason the infant takes delight in their ability to move a pencil. Freedom is our ability to make things up for the sake of being able to do so.

Yet, it's precisely the make-believe aspect of the work that most people with bullshit jobs find the most infuriating. Working serves a purpose, or is meant to do so. Being forced to pretend to work just for the sake of working is an indignity, since the demand is perceived as the pure excercise of power for its own sake.

If make-believe play is the purest expression of human freedom, make-believe work imposed by others is the purest expression of lack of freedom.

The notion that certain categories of people really ought to be working at all times, even if there's nothing to do, and that work needs to be made up to fill their time, even if there's nothing that really needs doing, refers to people who are not free: prisioners and slaves.

Idleness as a sin

Normal human work patterns take the form of periodic intense burst of energy, followed by relaxation, followed by slowly picking up again toward another intense bout. For example, farming.This is how humans have always tended to go about necessary tasks if no one forces them to act otherwise.

In most human societies, men tend to try to monopolize the most exciting, dramatic kind of work and relegate the more monotonous and time-consuming tasks to women. The more patriarchal the society, the more power men have over women, the more this tends to be the case.

The same pattern tends to reproduce itself whenever one group is clearly in a position of power over another.

The main reason why work could remain so irregular was because it was largely unsupervised. If those on the bottom produced what was required of them, those on the top didn't really feel they should have to be bothered knowing what that entailed.

The message of the modern "If you have time to lean, you have time to clean" is not that you should be working but that you shouldn't be doing anything else.

One can find occasional warnings by 19th century plantation owners in South America or the Caribbean that it's better to keep slaves busy even at made-up tasks than allow them to idle in the off-season: if slaves were left with time on their hands, they were likely to start plotting to flee or revolt.

The modern morality of "You're on my time, I'm not paying you to lounge around" is very different. It's the indignity of a man who feels he's being robbed.

A worker's time is not their own, it belongs to the person who bought it.

If an employee is not working, they're stealing something for which the employer paid good money (or promised to do so). By this moral logic, it's not that idleness is dangerous. Idleness is theft.

A change in time

In societies without clocks, time is measured by actions rather than action being measured by time. Events follow a logical order, but they are not controlled by an abstract system. Time is not a grid against which work can be measured, because the work is the measure itself.

By 14th century, most European towns had created clock towers, usually founded and encouraged by the local merchant guild. These merchants developed the habit of placing human skulls on their desks as memento mori (a reminder that they should make good use of their time because each chime of the clock brought them one hour closer to death).

By the late 1700s, time was simultaneously a fixed grid and a possession. Everyone was encouraged to see time as the medieval merchant did: as a finite property to be carefully budgeted and disposed of, much like money.

Also, the new technologies allowed any person's fixed time on earth to be chopped up into uniform units that could be bought and sold for money. The careful budgeting of time was the essence of morality.

Modern work discipline and capitalist techniques of supervision have their origins in merchant ships and slave plantations in the colonies that were later imposed on the working people back home. But the new conception of time was what made it possible, it was a technological and moral change

Over the course of the 18th and 19th centuries, starting in England, the middle classes came to see the poor as poor largely because they lacked time discipline: they spent their time recklessly, just as they gambled away their money

At the same time, workers of the Industrial Revolution began rebelling against oppressive conditions using the same language of time. The very act of demanding "free time" had the effect of reinforcing the idea that when a worker was "on the clock", their time truly did belong to the person who bought it.

  • If you're on the clock, don not be too efficient. You will not be rewarded, not even acknowledged
  • Instead, you'll be punished with meaningless bussy work

Being forced to pretend to work is the most absolute indignity: it's pure degradation, a sheer exercise of the boss' power for its own sake

The idea that dutiful sumbission even to meaningless work under another's autority is a form of moral self-discipline that makes you a better person. What "proper work ethic" means is obedience.

Even members of the public are encouraged to see themselves as bosses and to feel indignant if public servants (like transit workers) seem to be working in a casual way, let alone just lounging around
